Concrete Results Thus Far

  1. Faculty-Student Lunches. As a freshman, I proposed and spearheaded an initiative that lets you set up a free lunch in Schatz with a faculty member at any time during the semester. It's still going on! We have some unparalleled access to world-class faculty and I want to foster a culture of close faculty-student mentorships. This was on my platform last year, and now I'm working on making a permanent progmam.
  2. Academic Affairs White Paper. I initiated a system where we'll provide actionable suggestions for improvements to policies and practices surrounding academics to people who are best equipped to make the changes. This semester, we're focusing on the interplay between diversity and academics and the registration process. We'll share them with the administration and you; keep a lookout in the Tartan!
  3. CMUStories. For Senate Mental Health Week, I spearheaded cmustories, an initiative geared at starting a conversation about mental health through anonyously-shared, hard-hitting and unfiltered experiencess. Keep a lookout for the stories around campus, but here are the raw stories.
  4. Nightline. I introduced the idea of an anonymous peer hotline in my platform last year, and in the meantime my committee has been looking into its feasibility at CMU. I'm excited to say that we're about ready to move forward with implementation!
  5. Senator Office Hours. At my suggestion, Senate will be holding "office hours" in high-traffic areas of campus. Be on the lookout for SCS senators on Gates 5 soon!
  6. How-to's. I've written many multiple-page how-to's for future Senate chairs to replicate the success we've seen this semester.
  7. Wikipedia. I led an initiative to edit the Carnegie Mellon Wikipedia page to enhance our reputation – originally, it was pretty bad.
  8. Benchmarking. I was on the ad-hoc benchmarking committee we set up to find initiatives that could apply to CMU and that could make Stugov more efficient. I spoke in depth with over 8 leaders of student government at Princeton, Cornell, and Duke. We ended up working on a lot of the ideas I brought back.
  9. Interfacing with administration. I've interfaced with the majority of the senior administration at CMU during bi-weekly meetings, trustees' meetings, and things like the President's Dinner. I brought up real and honest student concerns that really surprised some of them.
  10. Senate Reports I've written. Take a look at my F15 Final Reports and my senate week report.

Moving Forward

  • I'd like to create an automatic feedback and complaint pipeline that will aggregate concerns and send them to the relevant people who can do something about it. It'll be as automatic as possible so that bureaucracy doesn't set into place.
  • Next semester, I want to administer an undergraduate-wide academic survey through Institutional Research and Analysis.
  • Senate should be more accountable and open. That means creating automatic feedback and reporting systems so we're beholden to you.
